What is Domain Age and Why It Matters

      

        Domain age refers to the length of time since a domain name was first registered. Older domains are often considered more trustworthy by search engines and users alike. They can have an advantage in rankings, as longevity signals stability and reliability.
      

      

        Many SEO professionals emphasize the importance of domain age as a ranking factor. While it is not the only factor, a domain with a longer history can benefit from established backlinks, trust, and recognition.
      


      
How Does Domain Age Affect SEO?

      
    
        
  • Trustworthiness: Older domains tend to be viewed as more credible by search engines.
    • 
        
  • Backlink Profile: Older domains often accumulate more backlinks over time, which enhances authority.
    • 
        
  • Indexing: Established domains are indexed more quickly and frequently.
    • 
        
  • Brand Recognition: A long-established domain name may be more recognizable to users.
