What is a Subdomain?

A subdomain is a prefix added to a main domain name, allowing an organization to create separate sections or services on their website. For example, in blog.example.com, "blog" is a subdomain of "example.com".

Benefits of Subdomain Enumeration for Security

Identifying subdomains can help security professionals find potentially vulnerable entry points and services that may require patching or monitoring.

How Subdomains Affect SEO

Subdomains are treated as separate websites by search engines, so understanding their existence helps you manage SEO strategy and avoid duplicate content issues.

Common Uses of Subdomains

  • Hosting blogs (e.g., blog.example.com)
  • Support or helpdesk portals (e.g., support.example.com)
  • Staging or testing environments (e.g., staging.example.com)
  • Regional websites (e.g., us.example.com)
